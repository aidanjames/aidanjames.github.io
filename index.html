Tour rules

  • The 2023 MGA Tour will conclude at 11:59 PM on 31 October, 2023.
  • Players may submit as many valid rounds, played in the stableford format and following standard golf rules, as they wish before the competition end date.
  • These rounds must be witnessed by another MGA tour player to be considered valid.
  • The player's total score will be determined by the sum of their top 5 stableford point rounds.
  • The individual with the highest total score at the competition end date will be declared the winner and will be treated to a dinner paid for by the losers. There is no second place prize.

Player profiles

CAMERON

Cameron (AKA Sven) is widely regarded as one of the most improved golfers in the world over the 12 months despite his ladies clubs and trolley. Pundits suggest he may end up with a handicap that he can't live up to. Nevertheless, a strong favourite to take the spoils. Celebrated winner of the last "Olympic" Sandy crack major, held in Brighton in the summer of 2020. He is never short of a layer of clothing.

HATTIE

Hattie is the only competitor in the field to never have won a Sandy Crack major, despite a couple of podium appearances. He hit a personal best score of 85 in 2022 but you probably already knew that. He plays left handed although the evidence suggests he's got this wrong. An outside chance of winning due to his generous handicap. Family commitments may hinder his chances.

PENDLEBURY

Pendlebury is a massive disappointment. Since winning the 2014 Sandy Crack open ("Olympic edition") he's been a consistent loser. Has not made a podium finish in as long as anyone can remember and recent form doesn't bode well. That said, some experts suggest he is due and could be a dark horse to take out both the MGA tour and the Sandy Crack open in 2023.
